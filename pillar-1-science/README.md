## Core Mission

**From isolated ivory towers to collaborative knowledge commons.**

Traditional science suffers from:
- Publication bias (only "positive" results get published)
- Lack of reproducibility (70% of studies can't be replicated)
- Gatekeeping by journals (knowledge locked behind paywalls)
- Incentive misalignment (publish-or-perish vs. truth-seeking)
- Slow peer review (months to years)
- Data siloing (proprietary datasets)

**The Science Pillar solves this by:**
- Anchoring all research on distributed ledgers (immutable, timestamped)
- Making peer review transparent and fast
- Rewarding data contribution and verification
- Creating a permanent, open Knowledge Commons
- Aligning incentives with scientific integrity

## Example Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Climate Science Rapid Response
**Scenario:** Urgent research on climate tipping points

**Traditional System:**
- Submit to journal: 6-12 months
- Peer review: 3-6 months
- Published: 12-18 months later
- **Total:** 2 years from discovery to public knowledge

**Science Pillar:**
- Submit to Open Science DLT: Instant timestamping
- Peer review: 1-2 weeks (transparent, DAO-managed)
- Published to Knowledge Commons Wiki: Immediately
- **Total:** 2 weeks from discovery to validated public knowledge

### Use Case 2: Traditional Ecological Knowledge Validation
**Scenario:** Indigenous community shares traditional knowledge of soil regeneration

**Traditional System:**
- Academic "extraction" (researchers take knowledge, get credit)
- Community not compensated
- Knowledge often misrepresented
- No ongoing relationship

**Science Pillar:**
- Community uploads knowledge to Wiki (with permission protocols)
- Receives SCI-EXPLORER tokens (ownership recognized)
- Researchers cite and build on knowledge (transparent attribution)
- Community earns SCI-REGEN tokens as research proves efficacy
- Long-term partnership model

### Use Case 3: Reproducibility Crisis
**Scenario:** Important medical study can't be reproduced

**Traditional System:**
- Reproduction attempts discouraged (not "novel")
- Journals won't publish negative results
- Original researchers face no consequences
- Bad science lingers in literature

**Science Pillar:**
- Reproduction attempts earn SCI-GUARDIAN tokens (incentivized)
- All reproduction attempts published (positive or negative)
- Original researchers' reputation adjusted based on reproducibility
- Wiki flags non-reproducible studies
